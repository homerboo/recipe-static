Yield: 4 servings

Prep Time: 15 mins

Total Time: 15 mins

Special Equipment: Blender

Difficulty: Easy

Shopping List

  • 1/4 lb ground beef
  • Scallions
  • Fermented chili bean paste
  • Xiaoxing wine
  • Knob ginger
  • 1.5 lb firm tofu
  • Dark Soy Sauce
  • Roasted chili oil
  • Sichuan peppercorns
  • Vegetable oil
  • Cornstarch
  • Bulb garlic
  • Low-sodium chicken stock

Preparation

5 prep bowls
  1. 3 cloves garlic grated on a microplane grater
  2. 1 tsp cornstarch & water
  3. 1/4 C chili oil & half 1/4C scallions, finely sliced
  4. Half the 2 tbsp peppercorns
  5. 2tbsp chili bean paste, 2 tbsp xioxing wine, 1/4 C chicken stock, 1 tbsp soy sauce

Directions

  1. Heat half of sichuan peppercorns in a large wok over high heat until lightly smoking. Transfer to a mortar and pestle. Pound until finely ground and set aside
  2. Add remaining sichuan peppercorns and vegetable oil to wok. Heat over medium high heat until lightly sizzling, about 1 1/2 minutes. Pick up peppercorns with a wire mesh skimmer and discard, leaving oil in pan.
  3. Combine corn starch and cold water in a small bowl and mix with a fork until homogenous.
  4. Bring a medium saucepan of water to a boil over high heat and add tofu. Cook for 1 minute. Drain in a colander, being careful not to break up the tofu.
  5. Heat oil in wok over high heat until smoking. Add beef and cook, stirring constantly for 1 minute.
  6. Add garlic and ginger and cook until fragrant, about 15 seconds.
  7. Add chili-bean paste, wine, soy sauce, and chicken stock and bring to a boil.
  8. Pour in corn starch mixture and cook for 30 seconds until thickened.
  9. Add tofu and carefully fold in, being careful not to break it up too much.
  10. Stir in chili oil and half of scallions and simmer for 30 seconds longer.
  11. Transfer immediately to a serving bowl and sprinkle with remaining scallions and toasted ground Sichuan pepper. Serve immediately with white rice.

Notes

You can use store-bought roasted chili oil, or make your own by toasting a cup of whole hot dried Chinese peppers in a wok until lightly charred, then adding 1 1/2 cups of vegetable or canola oil. Heat the oil until the chilis start to bubble slightly, then allow to cool and transfer to a sealable container. Chili oil will stay good in the refrigerator for several months.

I usually use freshly ground pepper rather than go through steps 1 & 2 and simply add it to the cooking ground beef
