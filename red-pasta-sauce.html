Yield: 2 quarts / 2.3 litres

Prep Time: 25 mins

Cook Time: 360 mins

Total Time: 385 mins

Special Equipment: Dutch Oven

Difficulty: Easy

Shopping List

  • 4 (28-ounce) cans whole peeled tomatoes, preferably imported D.O.P. San Marzano tomatoes
  • Extra-virgin Olive Oil
  • Butter
  • 2 bulbs garlic
  • Red pepper flakes
  • Dried oregano
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 1 medium onion
  • Basil
  • Fish Sauce
  • Peppercorns
  • Parsley (optional)

Preparation

4 prep bowls
  1. Grab the largest prep bowl you have, place tomatoes in the bowl, using your hands, crush the tomatoes by squeezing them in your fingers until pieces no larger than 1/2-inch remain
  2. Transfer 3 cups of crushed tomatoes to a sealed container and reserve in the refrigerator until Direction step 12
  3. 8 cloves of minced garlic
  4. 1 to 2 tsp red pepper flakes and 1 to 2 tsp dried oregano
  5. Remaining tomaotes from step 1, carrot quartered, onion split in half

Directions

  1. Adjust oven rack to lower position and preheat to 300°F (165°C)
  2. Heat olive oil and butter over medium heat in a large Dutch oven until butter is melted
  3. Add garlic and cook, stirring, until softened and fragrant but not browned, about 2 minutes
  4. Add pepper flakes and oregano and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 1 minute
  5. Add non-reserved tomatoes, carrot, onion, and basil, and stir to combine
  6. Season lightly with salt and pepper
  7. Bring to a simmer over high heat
  8. Cover Dutch oven with lid slightly ajar and transfer to oven
  9. Cook, stirring once every 1 to 2 hours, until reduced by about half and darkened to a deep red, 5 to 6 hours (reduce oven temperature if the sauce is bubbling too rapidly or the browned bits begin to turn too dark)
  10. Remove from oven
  11. Using tongs, discard onion halves, carrots, and basil stems
  12. Add reserved tomatoes to sauce and stir to combine, add fish sauce
  13. Season generously with salt and pepper and stir in minced herbs along with additional olive oil as desired

Notes

Refrigerate for up to 1 week or freeze in sealed containers for up to 6 months

I prefer charring the split onion separately with a cast iron pan before step 4

To reheat, warm very gently in a saucepan with 1/2 cup water, stirring until it all melts and heats through
