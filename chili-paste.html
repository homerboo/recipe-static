Yield: ~100 grams / 7 servings in Ma Po Tofu

Prep Time: 0 mins

Unattended Time: ~ 35 mins

Total Time: 35 mins

Special Equipment: Blender

Difficulty: Easy

Shopping List

  • 100 grams Dried red chilies

Preparation

No prep

Directions

  1. 100g long mild dried red chillies, can use hot, may want to remove seeds
  2. Fill a large sauce pan with water (with enough room for expansion) and chillies
  3. Bring the water to a boil and place perforated plate on the top so the chillies donâ€™t float up too much
  4. Once the water comes to a boil, cover lower heat and simmer for 10 mins
  5. Open and increase heat to high to strong boil for 1 min
  6. Cover and turn off heat allowing 30 mins to cool and plump up the chillies
  7. Remove the chillies from water and place in blender without water
  8. Blend to a paste
  9. Once ground 1tbsp of vinegar and stir

Notes

You can use store-bought roasted chili oil, or make your own by toasting a cup of whole hot dried Chinese peppers in a wok until lightly charred, then adding 1 1/2 cups of vegetable or canola oil. Heat the oil until the chilis start to bubble slightly, then allow to cool and transfer to a sealable container. Chili oil will stay good in the refrigerator for several months.

I usually use freshly ground pepper rather than go through steps 1 & 2 and simply add it to the cooking ground beef
